American Amber Ale

American Amber Ale is name after the golden to amber colour this American version of English Pale Ale exhibits. The colour is derived from tbe use of caramel and crystal malt additions, which are roasted to provide amber beers with the colour, body and flavour many beer fans have come to appreciate. Falling under the ale beer type, amber ales ferment at warmer tempratures for what is typically a much shorter amount of time than lager style beers.

American Amber Ale is one of the most widely enjoyed styles throughout the United States and serves as a cornerstone style of the American craft brewing revolution. American ambers are darker in colour than their pale ale cousins, the presence of caramel and crystal malts lending a toasted, toffee flavour, along with the perception of a fuller body when compared to beers without such as malts. Amber beer showcases a medium-high to high malt character with medium to low caramel character derived from the use of roasted crystal malts. It is characterised by American-variety hops, which lend the amber ale notes of citrus, fruit and pine to balance the sweetness of the malt.
